Leverkusen Eye Ten Hag as Alonso Replacement

With Xabi Alonso set to depart Bayer Leverkusen for Real Madrid, the Bundesliga club is actively seeking a successor. Erik ten Hag, the former Manchester United and Ajax manager, has emerged as a leading candidate for the role.

Ten Hag, who has been out of management since his departure from Manchester United in October 2024, is reportedly in advanced talks with Leverkusen. His experience in European competitions and a track record of developing young talent make him an attractive option for the club. [talkSPORT]

During his tenure at Ajax from 2017 to 2022, Ten Hag led the team to three Eredivisie titles and a memorable run to the UEFA Champions League semi-finals in 2019. His tactical acumen and emphasis on attacking football align with Leverkusen's playing philosophy. [Wikipedia]

Leverkusen's sporting director, Simon Rolfes, is keen to appoint a manager who can build upon the foundations laid by Alonso. The club's recent success, including a historic Bundesliga title, has raised expectations, and Ten Hag's profile fits the bill. [Bavarian Football Works]
