Álvaro Carreras Joins Real Madrid from Benfica

Real Madrid has made an exciting move in the transfer market by securing the services of talented left-back Álvaro Carreras from Portuguese giants Benfica. The deal is reportedly in the range of €40–50 million, with Manchester United set to receive a portion of the fee due to a previous buy-back clause.

Carreras has been a standout performer for Benfica in the 2024–25 season, contributing 4 goals and 5 assists in 47 appearances across all competitions. His dynamic playing style and attacking instincts have caught the attention of top European clubs, with Real Madrid winning the race for his signature.

The 22-year-old Spanish defender is no stranger to the Real Madrid system. He spent formative years at the club's academy, La Fábrica, between 2017 and 2020, before moving on to further develop his skills at other clubs. His return to the Santiago Bernabéu marks a homecoming of sorts, bringing back a player with both potential and familiarity with the club's culture.

Under new manager Xabi Alonso, Real Madrid is looking to build a squad capable of competing across multiple fronts, and Carreras fits perfectly into this vision. Alonso favors full-backs who can attack as well as defend, and Carreras’s versatility and attacking prowess make him an ideal addition to the team.

Real Madrid’s current options at left-back have been stretched in recent seasons, and Carreras's arrival will provide much-needed depth and competition. His ability to overlap, deliver precise crosses, and contribute to the attack is expected to enhance the team’s offensive options from the back.

Carreras’s defensive awareness and work ethic also add balance to the squad. He is known for his tenacity in duels, positional intelligence, and ability to recover quickly in transition, which will be vital in La Liga and Champions League matches.

Beyond his on-pitch abilities, Carreras is seen as a long-term investment for the club. At just 22 years old, he has room to grow and develop into a world-class left-back under the guidance of Real Madrid’s coaching staff. The club’s focus on nurturing young talent is a key part of their strategy, and Carreras is a prime example of this approach.

Financially, the deal also makes sense for Madrid. With Carreras having spent part of his youth career at the club, they are entitled to training compensation, which reduces the overall financial impact of the transfer.

Fans are excited to see Carreras don the iconic white jersey again, as he brings energy, skill, and a winning mentality. The hope is that he can follow in the footsteps of great Madrid left-backs and establish himself as a key figure in the team's future success.

The move also strengthens the club’s Spanish core, with Carreras joining a squad that blends experienced stars with emerging domestic talents. His addition further highlights Real Madrid’s commitment to building a team that is both competitive and representative of Spanish football excellence.

With the new season approaching, all eyes will be on Carreras as he looks to make an immediate impact in La Liga and the Champions League. His journey from La Fábrica to Benfica and now back to Madrid is a testament to hard work, development, and belief in one’s abilities.
